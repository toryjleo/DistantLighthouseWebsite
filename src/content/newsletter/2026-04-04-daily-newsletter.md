## Customer A
Customer A wants a personal website to establish her brand as a unique entity. In addition to your average run-of-the-mill website, she wants a checkout system that automates transactions and gives her spreadsheets of custmer and vendor info. She also wants something to help her in the shop: an app that auto-sync photos on her phone to her site and customers that are on between 2:00 and 4:00 AM PST are greeted by a little dancing T-Rex on the checkout page. 

## Customer B
Customer B stocks factories with machines, ALL over the state. Daily, they have workers delivering machines, taking parts to repair machines, and interfacing with customers across county lines. He wants to know what is on the docket for the day and what he has in his shop.
For Customer B, a calendar with daily deliveries is the main screen of the app. He can browse, punch in all the info for a delivery (down to the manager on-call for the day and his phone number) and assign a delivery to a worker in his system. A worker can open the app the day of work to see the address. Touching the "map" button opens the user's default map app and hits a trigger in the app to notify Customer B that his worker is on their way to the job.

Basically if you have some sort of **system** that needs to be made, we can make it.

At Distant Lighthouse we want technology to **strengthen** connections and build convenience. Not the other way around.
